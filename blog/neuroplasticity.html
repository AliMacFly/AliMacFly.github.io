Neuroplasticity

How does neuroplasticity relate to learning

The brain can evolve throughout its life and adapt to acquiring new skills. Neuroplasticity is the ability to rewire your brain, making it stronger or weaker depending on how you use it. Neuroplasticity can help the brain repair itself and expand its learning potential. Synapses’ can grow from reoccurring use or disappear from barely using the connections between the synaptic pathways. A synapse is the muscle connection between neurons that’s why cognitive learning is important for brain growth.

How does neuroplasticity benefit people

Since we now understand that a skill can be lost, practicing a task every day or over time will help develop long-term memory and a stronger synapse. After an injury neuron connection can be lost, but the neuro paths will regenerate through relearning or training.


    Principles in neuroplasticity

    There are 10 principles of how to make the most out of neuroplasticity.

    Use it or lose it –

  • A lack of motor skills can eventually lead to physical muscles being useless. Physical and mental activities are important to hold onto your full body and brain capability.

    • Age –

  • Younger brains are easily and more adaptable to learning, that’s why it’s important to teach children the right things at an early age a negative trait can linger from early development.

    • Repetition –

  • Regular practice is essential for neuroplasticity; repetition leads to strong neuro connection.

    • Interference –

  • The brain’s ability for changing and adapt to new environments or skills, such as learning two languages back-to-back the neuro pathways will interfere with one another making it harder to learn a new skill to its full potential, but if you separate your crafts into different days, it’s less like to clash with connecting pathways.

    • Salience –

  • A significant experience such as an accident can cause emotional salience, it can lead to long-lasting fear and stress from memory and increased activity in the amygdala and the hypothalamus. This principle helps determine to enhance cognitive abilities with emotions or meaningful learning experiences.

    • Transference or Generalization –

  • A change of environment can make learning a skill more effective. Applying new context to learning the skill gives the brain adaption in a variety of situations.

    • Intensity -

  • The brain responds to challenges and more hours into learning a new subject. With high focus and effort, there is a higher chance of faster learning and understanding of the subject.

    • Time –

  • It takes time for information to settle in your memory. Continued practice of a task creates an improvement in abilities and when you take a long break, for example, 6 months there is a possibility of a decline in the abilities that you may want to improve on.

    • Specificity –

  • The value of tailoring to learning a task and adapting to new skills to the individuals, as they can enhance the neuronal connections and brain function.

    • Use and Improve –

  • Enhancing learning experiences by using and improving earlier training and learning journey.

Conclusion - How to increase your neuroplasticity

To grow new neuro connections and increase your ability to learn, is repletion and challenge yourself once you get comfortable. Time can also be important when it comes to neuroplasticity to get the best out of your learning, only take short breaks enough to reflect but if you don’t practice the physical task, it can deteriorate the neuro connection and be able to complete the task correctly.


Growth Mindset

What is growth mindset

A growth mindset is a way of developing your skills through dedication and learning. Psychologist Carol Dweck come up with a theory that our brains are not fixed, but they are constantly evolving and growing. Growth mindset is embrace challenges and to view failure as a learning opportunity. Persistance is key, to grow your skill practice is a necessory task to achieve goals.
